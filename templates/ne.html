Kedarnath Yatra

About Kedarnath Yatra

The Kedarnath Yatra is one of the most revered pilgrimages in Hinduism. Located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, Kedarnath Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The temple is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level, near the Mandakini River.

The yatra typically takes place from April to November, during which thousands of devotees undertake the challenging trek to seek blessings from Lord Shiva. The temple remains closed during the winter months due to heavy snowfall.

Travel Tips

  • Ensure you are physically fit for the trek, as the journey can be strenuous.
  • Carry warm clothing, as temperatures can drop significantly, especially at night.
  • Book your accommodation in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.
  • Stay hydrated and carry sufficient water and snacks for the trek.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions while visiting the temple.
